The Idea The bulb hangs limply, darkness becomes the sole adornment in an otherwise empty room. How many hands have touched this once luminous jewel? Has a procession of people with blonde hair or any other unfairly perceived harem of paired wits been met in the roomâ€™s dimness with the same sense of uncertainty that radiates like a fountain from without? Yesterday's diamond is reduced to empty glass, flitting limbs suppose that an approximation is passable when lacking my exact whereabouts. The idea is a single tooth in an otherwise mute mouth, as untouchable as an overhead bat circumscribed by the moon's silhouette. I immolate my hair. The self-seriousness of this reductive act could hardly pass for artful thought. I am left with a burnt scalp and a friar's haircut.
